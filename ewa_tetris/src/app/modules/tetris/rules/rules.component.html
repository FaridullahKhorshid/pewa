The rules

The Goal of Tetris

The goal of Tetris is to score the most points by burning rows. The game ends when a block touches the top of your screen, so make sure you survive to rack up more points!


Points

Points are gained by clearing horizontal rows from your grid. To clear a row, you need to make sure there is no empty space left in the row. Clearing a row is called burning. You can burn 1, 2, 3 or 4 rows simultaneously. Burning 4 rows at once is called tetris. The more lines you burn at once, the more points you get.


The Blocks

Blocks come in 7 different shapes. The I-block, O-block, S-block, Z-block, J-block, T-block and L-block. All blocks can be moved left and right, and can be rotated clockwise and counter-clockwise to fit them in a line.


Controls

Use the left and right arrows on the keyboard to move the block. Z rotates the button counter-clockwise. X rotates the button clockwise. The down arrow makes your block drop faster.


The Winner

The winner is the player that has the most points when both players are game over.

If you have less points than your opponent and they game over, the game will continue until you have either racked up more points than they or you are game over as well.
